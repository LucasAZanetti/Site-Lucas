THE ULTIMATE BOOK SELECTION BY LUCAS AUGUSTO

This is a selection of the best books ever read by Lucas. That's it, and your opinion is not welcome because of your innate stupidity :)

CRIME & PUNISHMENT

Published in 1866, Crime & Punishment is the most celebrated work of Dostoievski. In this book, Raskolnikov, a poor and desperate young student, walks through the streets of Saint Petersburg until committing a crime who he will try to justify by a teory: great mens, as Cesar and Napoleon, were acquitted by History. This act results in a narrative full of labyrinths, fear and anxiety. You will be transported to the inside of his disturbed mind, you will fear his fears, feel his fevers. His feelings as revolt, hate, regret and pain. You will hate those who he hates, those people who pass through his life, day by day, people who fight to preserve they dignity against lots of forms of tyranny.
Some other titles by Dostoievski I can recommend are "Notes from Underground" and "The Double". Both of them will portrait the problems of human mind as anxiety, fear and retire from the world, paranoids and insecurity
(Crime e Castigo, Fiodor Dostoievsky)

LA CHUTE

Before I say something about the book per se, I've to talk about the author and his work as one. His philosophy is, for sure, one of the best I could be in touch with. Even his novels are full of philosophy, pain and questions about the human situation. I recommend not only this title, but all of them.
In "La Chute", a French lawyer makes his conscience exam in a sailor's pub, in Amsterdam. The narrator, self-denominated "pertinet-judge", expose the human nature mixed to a pity self criticism process. The man who speaks in "La Chute"gives himself a calculated confession. But where does the confession start and where does the accusation start? He isolated himself from the world after witness a suicide of a woman in the waters of the Sena.
(A Queda, Albert Camus)

NOUS N'AVONS JAMAIS ÉTÉ MODERNES

This is the first book I read from Bruno Latour, a great mind from philosophy, a great mind from the mankind, who left us this month (10/2022).
In this book-manifesto, Latour try to reconfigure the "modern" traditional division between nature and culture, trying to understand the hybrid ones whom are increasingly in our society, as the ecological disasters, the genetically modified organism and the robot with artificial intelligence.
(Jamais Seremos Modernos, Bruno Latour)

RECUSA DO NÃO LUGAR

In the words of the critic Roberto Taddei, "that proposes and inaugurates a new path for literature that, from the dialogue with psychoanalysis and philosophy, expands without being a tributary of them". If in his previous books the author-character isolated himself in a kind of romanticism of negativity, here JP makes a leap: the idea of ​​the subject who was born “outside”, estranged from the world – and, for that very reason, privileged to talk about it –, loses its aura in the name of welcoming, of the possibility of entering and resonating with the world. This turn is based on Sloterdijk's philosophy of the spheres, in contrast to the negativity and emptiness characteristic of Heidegger's or Nietzsche's thought, which marked the poet's prose- philosopher so far. Refusal of Non-Place is a moving (and far from self-indulgent) account of abandonment, seclusion and successive failed attempts to enter the world, combined with sophisticated philosophical and psychoanalytic concepts.
(Recusa do não-lugar, Juliano Garcia Pessanha)

SHARK DRUNK

Two men in a little boat. A monster in the depths below. This is the starting point of The Book of the Sea. From the tiny island of Skrova in the Lofoten Islands, the sea is examined throughout history, in storytelling, science, poetry and mythology. Hav er opphav, «the sea is origin» and shelters the most fascinating life forms. For the author, the sea becomes a constant source of rapture, new insights and adventures that leave the reader with a taste of salt. But The Book of the Sea is also a reflection on the natural history of man, who managed to map the entire world and navigate among the stars, but seems to maintain an obsession with the myth of the monster, perhaps due to an atavistic predatory instinct or fear of the unknown that the sea still awakens us today.
The book counts with great references, as the mention of legendary guitar player Rober Fripp, founder of classic prog-rock band King Crimson and of various of important pieces of the music scenario since he started until nowdays.
(O Livro do Mar, Morten Stroksnes)

ON THE HEIGHTS OF DESPAIR

Written in Romanian when the author was 22 years old (later he would write in French), it is an explosion of anguish and lyricism, which brutally and overwhelmingly expresses the pain of existing.Winner of the Young Writers Award, the book is in part fruit of the readings of Nietzsche, Schopenhauer, Bergson, Pascal and Dostoevsky. But, beyond these erudite influences, Nos cumes do despair is the result, above all, of his experience with the harsh lucidity of insomnia, with the harshness of uninterrupted vigil, that nothing without truce. From there, Cioran postulated the uselessness of philosophy and discovered the relief of the disenchanted word, without which he would have put an end to his nights. In the heights of despair he anticipates not only the themes? cosmic nihilism, skepticism and lucid and radical meditation on the inconvenience of being born? but also much of the style that would consecrate him.
(Nos Cumes do Desespero, Emil Cioran)

ROBINSON CRUSOE

Robinson Crusoe's basic argument is universally known. Isolated on his “Island of Despair” (off the coast of present-day Venezuela) after a tragic shipwreck, the English sailor fights for survival using all the scarce means at his disposal. With time and the tools recovered from the ship, he becomes a competent woodworker and farmer, as well as a goat herder and profound connoisseur of the Bible - the only available reading. Without contact with any human being for more than two decades, Crusoe one day saves a native from murder by cannibals who had landed on one of the beaches of the island, and soon makes him his servant, giving him the name of Friday. A few years later, chance brings an English ship near the island, starting a long conflict with the mutinous crew.
I can (and must) recommend the book "Vendredi ou les limbes du Pacifique", a certain kind of reinvention of Robinson Crusoe by the author Michel Tournier.
(Robinson Crusoé, Daniel Defoe/ Sexta-feira ou os Limbos do Pacífico, Michel Tournier)

LA INVENCION DE MOREL

The book tells the story of a fugitive who hides on an island, apparently deserted. Gradually, the character begins to discover the curious inhabitants of the island, falling in love with a young woman. Subsequently, he discovers the truth about the inhabitants, the story of the ghost ship found near the island and the invention of scientist Morel.
(A Invenção de Morel, Adolfo Bioy Casares)

INDUSTRIAL SOCIETY AND ITS FUTURE

Ok, that one is just for the real crazy ones. The author of the title is the famous Unabomber. In this manifesto, he totally goes crazy and declares his hate for modern society as cowards human-beings and destructors of nature with their problematic industries. Crazy man, but he has his good points.
(Sociedade Industrial e seu Futuro, Theodore Kaczynski)

SOFIES VERDEN

On the eve of her fifteenth birthday, Sofia Amundsen begins to receive rather strange notes and postcards. The notes are anonymous and ask Sofia who she is and where the world comes from. The postcards are sent from Lebanon, by an unknown major, to a certain Hilde Møller Knag, a girl whom Sofia does not know either. The mystery of tickets and postcards is the starting point of this fascinating novel. From chapter to chapter, from “lesson” to “lesson”, the reader is invited to go through the entire history of Western philosophy, while being involved in a thriller that takes a surprising turn.
(O Mundo de Sofia, Jostein Gaarden)
